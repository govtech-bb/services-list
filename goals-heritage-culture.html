Only 13 heritage/culture services found in the entire government service registry. This is the second-smallest goals page (only Clean Water with 9 services is smaller).

Essential services NOT found in registry:

  • Artist registration/support - No services for artist certification, grants, or professional development
  • Heritage site preservation - No services for historic site designation, restoration, or maintenance
  • Libraries & archives - No library cards, book borrowing, archive access, or research services
  • Creative industries support - No film industry, music industry, publishing, or craft/artisan programs
  • Cultural events support - Limited to basic permits; no festival grants, venue support, or event coordination
  • Intellectual property - No copyright registration, trademark services for cultural works
  • Heritage tourism - No cultural tourism programs or heritage trail development
  • Cultural education - No arts in schools programs, cultural workshops (beyond basic registration)

This severe lack of services makes achieving "growth of the heritage economy" extremely difficult.

Note: The 13 services shown include 3 staff administrative forms and 1 crime reduction program (not heritage-focused). Only ~9 services are genuinely citizen-facing heritage/culture services.
